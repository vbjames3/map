Mayor Adams Says the Upper East Side is Clean, Residents Say Otherwise

Despite having some of the cleanest streets in New York City, rat sightings multiplied in the Upper East Side.

by James Van Bramer

December, 14 2022

According to inspections by the Mayor’s Office for Operations, 0.2% of streets are the Upper East Side was considered “filthy.” In addition, none of the neighborhood’s sidewalks were ranked filthy. But the report states that about 1.5% of streets citywide are filthy.

But an analysis of 311 rat sightings complaints shows 745 rat sightings since the start of this year, just under three times more than in 2018 when there were 245 sightings

As the city faces an uphill battle in reversing the trend, Frank Pezzella, a John Jay College Associate Professor and hate crimes expert, says that "offenders have become much more emboldened" about perpetrating hate crimes during the recent spikes.
